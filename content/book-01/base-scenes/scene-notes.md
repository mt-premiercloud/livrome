# Scene notes — [NAME] and the Stars

Brief for the illustrator. Each page is 21×21 cm; think **single full-bleed panel** with room for 1–2 lines of text along the bottom edge (leave ~15% bottom margin clear). Our palette bible: `plum`, `plum-deep`, `honey`, `cream`, plus `ink` for line work. Use watercolor texture — the "gift-quality keepsake" feel we promised.

Hero is a **4–5 year old child** in simple cream pyjamas with a plum trim. Face is always drawn as a **smooth oval in flat skin-tone placeholder** (#E8C0A6 is fine for the reference — it gets replaced at composite time). Hair is minimal at the base — a very short, neutral cap; the pipeline may add hair overlay in later phases, but for launch we ship with base-cap only.

## Cover

**Composition**: front-view vertical portrait orientation though cover is square. Hero stands on rooftop, arms slightly open, long string of 8 honey stars trailing up from one hand into an indigo-plum sky. Moon upper-right, quarter-full. Title set in Fraunces italic across top third: *Les étoiles de [NAME]* / *[NAME] and the Stars*. Livrome mark bottom-center.
**Palette**: plum-deep sky, honey stars + moon, cream rooftop highlights, ink silhouette details.
**Hero pose**: ¾ front, slight lean back to look up. Face oval ~14% of frame width, centered on the upper-third horizontal.

## Page 1 — Evening window

Interior bedroom at dusk. Hero sits cross-legged on a small bed, blanket around shoulders, looking toward a tall narrow window at right. Moonlight enters in a honey-cream wedge on the floor. No other characters. Soft watercolor. **Mood**: quiet, awake. **Palette lead**: plum-deep for walls, cream for bed, honey for moonlight wedge.

## Page 2 — The big dark

Wider shot, same bedroom, pulled back. Hero is small-in-frame, tucked at the pillow. Ceiling dominates: textured plum-deep. Only 4–5 stars glint through the window glass. **Mood**: reverent, not ominous. Avoid heavy shadows; keep edges soft. **Palette lead**: plum-deep 70% of frame, sparse honey pinpricks.

## Page 3 — First star falls

One fist-sized honey-yellow star mid-bounce on the blanket, slight motion streak above. Hero in ¾ view, eyes wide, mouth slightly round (surprise — not fear). Hands still folded. A few tiny honey sparks drift off the star. **Mood**: wonder. **Palette lead**: cream blanket, plum pyjamas, honey star dominant.

## Page 4 — Hello

Close-up (knee-up shot). Hero leaning forward on all fours over the pillow. The tiny star (palm-sized now) sits on the pillow, glowing. Tiny face on the star — two dot eyes, soft half-smile. Warm honey halo around the star. **Mood**: intimate, almost conspiratorial. Hero's face is peaceful.

## Page 5 — A whole family

Room still visible but camera low, near the mattress. Six stars of varying sizes bounce across the bed, floor, nightstand. Two are stuck in the blanket folds. Hero sitting up, laughing quietly, one star cupped in both hands near chest. **Mood**: warm chaos. **Palette lead**: honey pops everywhere, plum framing.

## Page 6 — The decision

Hero sitting on edge of bed, feet not yet touching the floor. One star resting in right palm, looking up at hero. Hero's expression: resolved, chin up slightly. The rest of the stars gathered on the pillow behind, looking on. **Mood**: quiet bravery. Keep the line economy — few strokes, strong pose.

## Page 7 — Getting ready

Floor-level shot. Hero pulling a plum-deep coat over pyjamas. Boots waiting at left, stars peeking out of each boot (2 stars visible). Window behind hero slightly ajar, night visible. **Mood**: setting out. Include a small trailing scarf for character. **Palette lead**: plum-deep coat, cream pyjamas beneath, honey boot-tops.

## Page 8 — The window sill

Hero crouched at the open window, back toward viewer, arm extended. One star lifting up from their palm into the night sky. A single tree visible outside, silhouette against moon. Brass hinge detail on window frame. **Mood**: first step. **Palette lead**: plum-deep exterior, honey moon, cream interior walls.

## Page 9 — The garden

Night exterior. Hero in coat, boots, kneeling in a small garden. Releasing a star above a sleeping striped cat curled near tulips. Honeycomb-pattern garden path behind. House windows in distance with one warm lamp. **Mood**: tender, low-light. **Palette lead**: plum-deep sky, honey window + star + cat eye-shine, cream house highlights.

## Page 10 — Halfway

Cobblestone lane at night. Hero walking left-to-right, both hands cupped in front of chest holding 3 stars. Gas-style street lamps (2–3) recede into distance. Soft fog at ankles. **Mood**: purposeful. Low-key honey accents in the lamps match the stars. Include a small cat silhouette trailing 6 paces behind (callback to page 9).

## Page 11 — This way

Narrow stone stairway between tall townhouses. Stars hover just above the next step, glowing, marking the path. Hero looking up, one hand on banister. **Mood**: guided. Walls should feel warm-plum (not threatening). Keep perspective gentle — not vertiginous.

## Page 12 — The rooftop

Wide-angle. Hero standing on the peak of a pitched slate rooftop, arms slightly out for balance. Enormous sky — 75% of the frame is indigo-plum with scattered honey stars. Chimney at left with smoke curling up (not ominous — warm home smoke). Silhouette of village rooftops below. **Mood**: awe. **Palette lead**: plum-deep sky dominant, honey stars + chimney-smoke-edges.

## Page 13 — Thank you

Close-up of hero's open right palm, star lifting off, trailing a honey spiral upward. Small face on the star, still smiling. Hero's face in frame (¾ view), content. **Mood**: release. Keep the hand rendering specific — index finger slightly curved inward, warm skin-tone placeholder.

## Page 14 — The great sky

Hero bottom-center, very small, on a slight hilltop ridge. Sky fills 80% of frame. Hundreds of stars of varying sizes. A wisp of indigo cloud crosses the upper-third. The moon sits upper-right, three-quarter. **Mood**: cosmic. Avoid over-rendering the stars — varied size + subtle glow is enough.

## Page 15 — Brave

Tight portrait. Hero face fills ~45% of frame, looking directly at viewer. Face oval composited — the final image should read as the child's own face. Starlight in the irises (we'll add reflection at composite stage). **Mood**: calm, not smiling, not afraid. No background detail — just a soft graded plum-deep behind, with 2 star pinpricks near ears.

## Page 16 — Last crossing

Moonlit grassy hill. Hero walking up at a gentle angle. Grass rendered with loose brushwork. Two honey stars visible through the fabric of the coat pocket, glowing through the cloth. Moon large, low on horizon behind the hill. **Mood**: almost done. **Palette lead**: plum-deep sky, sage-green grass hint, honey pocket glow.

## Page 17 — At the top

Wide shot again. Hero silhouetted on the hilltop, arms at sides, looking up. Sky fills 85% of frame; ground 15%. Stars denser toward the upper third. One patch of clear dark between stars marks the "gap" the two remaining stars will fill. **Mood**: still, complete. Do not add any extra character or element — this is the visual breath before the release.

## Page 18 — Together

Close-up on hero's open upturned hands. Two honey stars rising in a tight helical trail upward, about 15 cm of streak above the hands. Hero face not in frame (just forearms + hands + sky above). **Mood**: farewell. Palette pure — plum-deep sky, honey trail.

## Page 19 — Whole again

Wide shot. Full star field — densely scattered honey stars. At the top-center, two conspicuously larger + brighter stars touching. Hero bottom-third, back to viewer, arms at sides, watching up. **Mood**: completion. Don't over-punctuate the two "home" stars; trust the reader to find them.

## Page 20 — Goodnight

Looking up from hero's point-of-view. Upper 70% of frame is sky with ~30 stars; lower 30% is a suggestion of hero's upturned face and shoulders (rim-lit). One small speech bubble-like honey glow near the star cluster carries the text visually. **Mood**: blessing. Make the rim light on the hero distinctly warm — honey catching on cheek, chin.

## Page 21 — The way home

Hero walking down the grassy hill, back to viewer, toward a small village below. One window lit warm in the middle distance. Path traces a gentle S-curve. Moon now setting at left edge. **Mood**: return. **Palette lead**: plum-deep sky beginning to soften to plum at horizon.

## Page 22 — Back in bed

Interior. Hero sliding under the blanket. Coat draped over the chair (same coat from page 7, visible). Two boots beneath. A single star glimmers on the windowsill outside. **Mood**: homecoming. **Palette lead**: cream blanket, plum walls, honey window-star.

## Page 23 — Eyes closing

Close-up, hero's sleeping face fills frame. Tiny satisfied smile — corners of mouth just up. Star mobile gently turns above (suggest with 3 soft blurred stars at top edge of frame). **Mood**: rest. This is the emotional payoff page — keep it slow, warm. Skin tone placeholder carefully centered in face oval.

## Page 24 — The last watcher

View **through** the bedroom window at night, from outside. Hero dimly visible asleep in bed through the glass. One bright star mid-frame in sky, with a soft honey halo and the faintest suggestion of a smile (two dot-eyes + small upturned line). **Mood**: watching over. No hero face oval on this page — pipeline skips face-swap when `hero_bounds: null`.

## Style bible (recap)

- **Line**: varied weight, hand-drawn feel. Never CAD-precise.
- **Texture**: watercolor wash, slight grain. Should feel printed, not rendered.
- **Light**: always one dominant source (moon, window, star). Never ambient-flat.
- **Palette**: 3–4 colors per page from the bible. Don't introduce new hues without noting in the manifest.
- **Faces**: hero face is an oval with a skin-tone flat fill. No eyes, no mouth, no nose at the base layer. The swap layer adds all features.
- **Negative space**: leave the bottom ~15% of each page relatively calm — that's where the typeset text goes.
- **Safety**: no hard edges at the bleed. Important hero features stay > 10 mm from the trim line.
